Design Challenge 1

Which two websites are you most inspired by?

FiftyThree - www.fiftythree.com/paper

Promethean IT - www.prometheanit.com

How do they exhibit effective use of space, size, or balance?

FiftyThree

  • Page is broken up into 6 frames as you scroll, each being the same size but with different grid column formats
  • Focal points are the headlines of each frame, well scaled with the background
  • First two frames have very little negative space, clustered animated graphics are balanced with large, centered text
  • More negative space is mixed in as you scroll down to lower frames, focusing attention on text and animations
  • Site encourages scrolling through each frame

PrometheanIT

  • Simple minimalist webpage, lots of open space, no images or graphics apart from 6 colored columns
  • Huge focal point is the centered company name/logo, effective use of space and color contrast to make it an interesting home page
  • Single frame homepage, minimal hierarchy, encourages clicking through each tab, which includes more nested click-throughs
  • Becomes a little boring, could use more focal points on each sub-page or more graphical content

What makes them similar and different?

Each page is very different from each other. Promethean is very simple, with lots of negative space and just a single frame to navigate through for almost the entire site. Promethean also has a consistent color pattern. FiftyThree, on the other hand, is relatively cluttered with graphics and animations, is highly animated and multi-frame. Both have interesting navigation features, but Promethean encourages clicking while FiftyThree encourages scrolling.

Thumbnail Sketches

The following sketches are for a potential book-centric personal blog.

  • Home Page Sketch 1

    • In this first sketch, I'm going for a large, centered picture and title as the focal point, surrounded by a lot of negative space. As you scroll down, I put a thin bar with links to additional pages that helps to frame the top picture/title. Below the bar, the hierarchy flow shifts to 2 columns of different sized, evenly spaced rectangles that will contain a picture. The arrangement of these pictures/posts are aimed to encourage exploration. At the bottom of each rectangle will be a transparent box with the title of the post.

  • Home Page Sketch 2

    • In this second sketch, I'm going for a single frame webpage that has a very large image and title on the left side of the screen and scrollable, uneven rectangles on the right side. These rectangles are similar to the ones in sketch 1 but will be smaller and the title will only show up if you scroll-over the images. I have also placed the links bar at the top of the screen. The focal point is heavily on the left side of the screen to the large image/title, but since the screen is symettrically cut in two, the right side of the screen will be the natural point of attention after the title.
